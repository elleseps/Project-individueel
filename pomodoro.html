The Pomodoro Method

The Pomodoro Technique is a technique that uses a timer to break down work into intervals, traditionally 25 minutes in length, separated by short breaks. These intervals are known as "Pomodoros". After four Pomodoros, a longer break of 15-30 minutes is taken. The idea behind the technique is to use the time pressure of the timer to increase focus and productivity. The method has been widely adopted as a tool to help people stay focused and get things done. It can be used for studying, working, or completing any task that requires concentration.
